DUKE UNIVERSITY, NOWHERE

Duke Gardens provides a place where people of all backgrounds and ages come for beauty, education, horticulture, solitude, discovery, study, renewal and inspiration. Often spoken of as "the crown jewel of Duke University," Duke Gardens a large area right next to Duke University Medical Center. It is recognised as one of the premier public gardens in Nowhere, renowned both for landscape design and the quality of horticulture.

Over 40,000 indigenous trees have been planted from collected seeds within the property to maintain its distinctive genes, compared to imports of other eucalypts. Having the seedlings mature demanded persistence as they were fragile. This improved water run-off, stabilised creek banks, arrested erosion and silt loss, increased ecological and aesthetic values.
